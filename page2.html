GTA 6 Map – Vice City, Leonida & the Largest World Rockstar Has Built

The GTA 6 map is officially set in Vice City and the surrounding state of Leonida. Rockstar confirmed this through the first official trailer, which showcases urban areas, beaches, highways, swamps, and rural regions inspired by modern-day Florida.

Unlike previous GTA titles, GTA 6’s world is designed to feel alive and constantly changing. Traffic density, NPC behavior, and environmental events dynamically shift based on location and time.

Vice City Returns

Vice City has been completely reimagined for GTA 6. It is no longer a stylized 1980s city but a modern metropolis reflecting social media culture, tourism, nightlife, and crime. Rockstar aims to parody contemporary American life more than ever before.

Leonida State Explained

Leonida is Rockstar’s fictional version of Florida. It includes coastal towns, wetlands, highways, and remote areas far beyond the city. This suggests a much larger playable area than GTA 5.

Is This the Biggest GTA Map Ever?

While Rockstar has not released exact measurements, developers confirmed the world is larger and more detailed than GTA 5. More importantly, it is denser, with more interior spaces and interactive locations.

Dynamic World Events

The trailer shows police raids, parties, wildlife encounters, and viral moments happening naturally across the map. These are not scripted cutscenes but part of the living world system.

Conclusion

GTA 6’s map is not just bigger—it is smarter, more immersive, and more realistic than any previous Rockstar world.
